Research focus

Physical-layer methods for 6G-era wireless

Four threads run through my work: shaping the propagation environment with intelligent surfaces, recovering channels efficiently, designing low-cost beamforming hardware, and sensing the world with millimeter waves.

01

Reconfigurable intelligent surfaces

Passive beamforming with practical and discrete phase-shift models, element-wise designs, and STAR-RIS — turning the channel itself into a design variable.

IRS / RISSTAR-RISdiscrete phasepassive beamforming
02

Sparse channel estimation

Compressed-sensing and Bayesian estimation for IRS-assisted OFDM, exploiting sparsity to cut pilot overhead while keeping recovery robust.

sparse recoverySBL / VBIOFDMimperfect CSI
03

Hybrid precoding & low-resolution hardware

Energy-efficient hybrid precoding with low-resolution phase shifters and limited RF chains for mmWave massive MIMO — performance that survives real hardware limits.

hybrid precodingmmWave MIMOlow-res phase shifterslimited RF chains
04

mmWave radar sensing & ISAC

Contactless FMCW-radar sensing with attention-guided deep learning — liquid identification & adulteration detection and health monitoring — alongside integrated sensing-and-communication directions for 6G.

FMCW radarcontactless sensingattention modelsISAC / 6G
